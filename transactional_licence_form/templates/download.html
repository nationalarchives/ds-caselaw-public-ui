What you need to apply for a licence

Last updated on 10th April 2024

Important

You do not need to apply for a licence if your re-use is already covered under the Open Justice licence.

There is one application form to apply to perform computational analysis across Find Case Law records. It is made up of 5 main sections. You will need to respond to all the questions we ask. There is no way to save the application part way through.

4. A public statement

We will ask you to write a brief public statement that summarises the reasons why you want to perform computational analysis. This statement should include

  • purpose and anticipated outcomes
  • individuals or communities served
  • methodologies or activities this will involve

You should aim for no more than 250 words in your public statement.

Responses to this section may be published in the future, for example if we receive a freedom of information (FOI) request.

5. Statements and principles

Statements and principles
! Information In the interests of transparency, we may publish your responses to some of the principles, alongside your public statement and details of your organisation in the future - we will tell you which responses, as you fill in the application process.
1. Dignity of the Courts

It is important that any analysis maintains the dignity of the courts and tribunals and does not undermine their functioning as working bodies. This is consistent with access to justice.

  1. Licence holders must not undermine the courts and tribunals’ ability to function as working bodies. Do you accept this licence term?
    • Yes
    • No
  2. Please describe
    a. the risks you have identified and
    b. how you will protect the ability for the courts and tribunals to function.
    You should consider anything that may undermine the ability for the justice system to operate impartially and with integrity and treat all members of the public equally and fairly, no matter who they are.
2. Independence of the Court

It is important that any analysis respects the independence of the judiciary and their impartial judgment.

  1. Licence holders must respect the independence of the judiciary. Do you accept this licence term?
    • Yes
    • No
  2. Please describe
    a. the risks you have identified and
    b. how you will protect the independence of the justice system
    You should consider anything that may undermine the ability for the justice system to be fair and transparent, free of any influence outside the rule of law.
3. Appropriate Scrutiny

We seek to encourage the analysis of machine-readable judgments to allow the public to scrutinise justice outcomes and the law more effectively. We also acknowledge that the incomplete nature of the dataset and the current lack of safeguard against opaque or biased methodologies increases the risk of inaccurate or biased conclusions which may cause harm to individuals named therein.

Apply Now

Please apply by downloading and completing application form (Microsoft Word 85.7 KB)

Once you have completed the application form, please save as an attachment and email The National Archives Licence team:caselawlicence@nationalarchives.gov.uk

It may take a few weeks until you receive a decision on your application. You can read more about the approval process on the licence application process page.
